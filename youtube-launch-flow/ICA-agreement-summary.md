# ICA Agreement Summary

## Document Reviewed
- Title: Independent Contractor Agreement (ICA)
- File: ICA_-_Master_-_Talent_tabot_charles_bessong_saugat_wasti.pdf
- Length: 7 pages
- Date seen in document: 11 April 2026

## Executive Summary
This agreement is heavily company-favorable. You are engaged as an independent contractor, expected to contribute about 16 hours per week, and compensated primarily through equity (up to 150,000 shares, stated as 1.5%).

Most important points are:
1. You are not an employee and receive no employee benefits.
2. You assign all project-related IP and waive moral rights.
3. Vesting depends partly on an MVP completion milestone controlled by the founder.
4. Liability and indemnity obligations are broad and potentially expensive for you.
5. Termination and payment wording is risky and needs revision.

## What The Agreement Says (Plain English)
1. Services and scope are defined by Exhibit A project descriptions.
2. You should dedicate around 16 hours weekly, with weekly reports and performance ratings.
3. Contract term is 6 months from start date, extendable in writing.
4. Compensation is equity-based:
- Up to 150,000 common shares (stated as 1.5%).
- 50% vests when MVP is complete.
- 50% vests after 6 months.
- Share price shown as $0.00001.
5. There is a conditional buyback concept:
- You and each hired talent may sell back up to $10,000 of shares.
- Only available after the company raises more than $150,000.
6. All work product tied to services becomes company property (work-for-hire + assignment).
7. Confidentiality obligations continue after termination.
8. Consultant indemnifies company for several claim types.
9. Either party can terminate on 30 days written notice.

## What To Watch Out For
1. No guaranteed cash compensation
- The document appears to provide equity but no salary/retainer.

2. MVP trigger is subjective
- Founder decides when MVP is complete, which controls 50% vesting.

3. Termination payment clause is unfavorable/unclear
- Wording suggests company may avoid paying even for completed work at termination.

4. Broad IP transfer and moral rights waiver
- You give up ownership and attribution rights over project outputs.

5. Broad indemnification exposure
- You may bear large legal costs for IP, negligence, and compliance-related claims.

6. Insurance obligations on consultant
- You must maintain adequate insurance at your own expense.

7. Inconsistent drafting quality
- Duplicate clauses, placeholders, and legal inconsistencies may create ambiguity.

8. Equity terms are incomplete
- Missing practical details such as plan documents, exercise windows, tax treatment, dilution, transfer restrictions, and exit/liquidity mechanics.

9. Buyback right is narrow
- Capped at $10,000 and dependent on fundraising threshold.

10. Execution/completion gaps
- Signature and party details appear partially blank in extracted text.

## What You Gain If It Works
1. Equity upside if the company grows significantly.
2. Formalized role and scope through Exhibit A.
3. A limited potential liquidity path through buyback language.
4. Flexibility of contractor structure.

## Recommended Revisions Before Signing
1. Add objective MVP completion criteria
- Define measurable acceptance criteria and an approval process not controlled by one person alone.

2. Fix payment on termination
- Ensure payment for all accepted or substantially completed work up to termination date.

3. Narrow and cap indemnity
- Exclude indirect losses and set a reasonable liability cap.

4. Clarify full equity mechanics
- Attach or reference option plan terms, vesting triggers, acceleration, exercise conditions, tax notes, and dilution expectations.

5. Add dispute and jurisdiction clarity
- Keep governing law, arbitration, and forum language consistent.

6. Complete all blanks and party details
- Ensure legal names, addresses, dates, signatures, and witness details are fully completed.

7. Add practical operations terms
- Include invoicing/expense reimbursement, acceptance process, reporting format, and communication cadence.

## Questions To Ask The Company
1. Is there any cash compensation, stipend, or expense reimbursement?
2. What exact criteria determine MVP completion?
3. What option/share plan documents govern this grant?
4. What happens to unvested and vested shares if contract ends early?
5. Are there restrictions on exercising or selling shares beyond the buyback clause?
6. Will indemnity be capped and limited to direct losses caused by proven breach?

## Final Take
As drafted, this ICA gives the company strong protection while leaving you with meaningful uncertainty around vesting, payment security, and liability exposure. It should be negotiated and cleaned up before signature.
